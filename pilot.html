Soaring Above the Clouds

Pilots navigate aircraft safely while transporting passengers and cargo across the globe.

Job Summary & Responsibilities

Airline pilots fly and navigate airplanes, helicopters, and other aircraft. Airline pilots typically transport people and cargo on a fixed schedule.

  • Check aircraft before flights (mechanical condition, fuel, equipment)
  • Operate aircraft according to flight plans and air traffic control instructions
  • Monitor engines, fuel consumption, and other systems during flight
  • Communicate with air traffic control during takeoff, flight, and landing
  • Respond to changing conditions and emergencies
  • Work schedule: Irregular hours including nights, weekends, and holidays
  • Work locations: Commercial airlines, cargo carriers, charter services

Top 5 Required Skills

Decision Making

Quick, sound judgments in emergency situations

Technical Skills

Understanding complex aircraft systems

Communication

Clear coordination with crew and ATC

Spatial Awareness

Strong sense of direction and positioning

Stress Management

Remaining calm under pressure

Education Requirements

  • Bachelor's degree (often in aviation, engineering, or related field)
  • FAA-approved flight school training
  • Airline Transport Pilot (ATP) certification requires 1,500 flight hours
  • Military flight training also a common path

Certification & Experience

Private Pilot License

40-60 flight hours minimum

Written and practical exams

Commercial Pilot License

250 flight hours minimum

Advanced flight training

Instrument Rating

Flying by instruments only

Required for most commercial jobs

Airline Transport Pilot License

1,500 flight hours minimum

Final step to airline captain

Job Outlook

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects:

  • 4% growth from 2022 to 2032 (about as fast as average)
  • 16,800 openings projected annually
  • Strongest demand in regional and cargo carriers
  • Potential pilot shortage due to mandatory retirements

Earning Potential

Median Annual Salary: $148,900 (May 2022)

Top 10% Earned: More than $239,200

Starting Salary (Regional): $50,000-$80,000

Major Airlines: $150,000-$300,000+ after seniority

Student Perspective

What I Like:

  • Exciting, non-desk job with travel opportunities
  • High earning potential at major airlines
  • Respected professional career
  • Constant learning and challenges

What I Don't Like:

  • Expensive training requirements
  • Irregular schedule and time away from home
  • High stress and responsibility
  • Slow career progression initially

Is This a Good Fit For Me?

This career would be a good fit if you have excellent spatial awareness, thrive under pressure, don't mind irregular schedules, and are passionate about aviation. It requires significant upfront investment in training but offers rewarding work and excellent compensation at senior levels.

Lifestyle Considerations

A pilot career can support:

  • Upper-middle class to wealthy lifestyle (depending on airline and seniority)
  • Frequent travel opportunities
  • Non-traditional work schedule with blocks of days off
  • Potential to live in base cities worldwide
  • High job satisfaction for aviation enthusiasts
