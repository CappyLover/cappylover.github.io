Designing the Future

Mechanical engineers create, analyze, and maintain mechanical systems that power our world.

Job Summary & Responsibilities

Mechanical engineers design, develop, build, and test mechanical and thermal devices including tools, engines, and machines. They work in various industries from automotive to aerospace.

  • Analyze problems to see how mechanical devices might help solve them
  • Design or redesign mechanical devices using CAD software
  • Develop and test prototypes of devices they design
  • Oversee manufacturing process for the device
  • Work schedule: Typically full-time, sometimes overtime to meet deadlines
  • Work locations: Offices, factories, laboratories, or construction sites

Top 5 Required Skills

Math Skills

Advanced mathematics including calculus and statistics

CAD Software

Proficiency in AutoCAD, SolidWorks, or similar

Creativity

Innovative problem-solving abilities

Teamwork

Collaboration with other engineers and professionals

Analytical Skills

Ability to analyze complex systems

Education Requirements

Most mechanical engineering positions require:

  • Bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering or mechanical engineering technology
  • ABET-accredited program preferred
  • Some positions may require a master's degree or PhD
  • Continuing education to keep up with technological advances

Certification & Experience

  • Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam for entry-level
  • Professional Engineering (PE) license for higher levels
  • Internships or co-op experience highly valuable
  • Specialized certifications in areas like HVAC, robotics, or automotive systems

Job Outlook

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics projects:

  • 2% growth from 2021 to 2031 (slower than average)
  • About 17,900 openings projected each year
  • Best opportunities for those with new technologies training
  • Strong demand in renewable energy and robotics fields

Earning Potential

Median Annual Salary: $95,300 (May 2021)

Student Perspective

What I Like:

  • Opportunity to work on diverse projects
  • Good salary potential
  • Ability to see tangible results of work
  • Continuous learning opportunities

What I Don't Like:

  • Can be stressful with tight deadlines
  • May require working in noisy environments
  • Need for continuing education
  • Some positions require extensive travel

Is This a Good Fit For Me?

This career would be a good fit if you enjoy solving practical problems, have strong math and science skills, and like working with both computers and physical systems. It offers stability, good pay, and opportunities to work in many different industries.

Lifestyle Considerations

A mechanical engineering career can support:

  • Middle to upper-middle class lifestyle
  • Stable work hours in most positions
  • Opportunities to live in urban or suburban areas
  • Potential for international work in some specialties
  • Good work-life balance in many positions
